OpenAQ-Transit

The first unified curb-to-cabin air-quality monitoring network in sub-Saharan Africa.

55production units
deployed in Nairobi & Kigali

02 — Hardware Architecture

  • Dual Sensors: Twin optical PM sensors on staggered duty cycles for in-field drift tracking.
  • Bus-Stop Units: 15W solar-powered nodes for city-scale exposure mapping.
  • Bus-Mounted Units: Powered by the vehicle rail, featuring PIR-triggered cameras for source attribution.
  • Connectivity: Data uploaded via cellular Notehub link with over-the-air firmware updates.

03 — Deployment & Results

The network enables evaluation of real-world impacts during diesel-to-electric bus transitions.

  • Achieved < 15% bias in 14-day calibration co-location tests.
  • 99% data-sync reliability across the 55-node production run.
  • Reduces PM sensor duty-cycle by 88% while maintaining accuracy.
